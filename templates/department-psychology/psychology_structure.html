Program Structure

BS Psychology

The BS Psychology is 8 semesters (4 years) program offered by the department of management and Social Sciences. As per the HEC guidelines, the curriculum has been structured around a set of compulsory courses, general courses, foundation courses, major courses, and electives courses.


Program Outcomes

At the time of graduation, students will be able to

  1. Understand goals, methods of inquiry, and areas of study that characterize the discipline using critical thinking, analytical skills, and experimentation.
  2. Identify various schools of thought in psychology, their history, and critically evaluate their differences.
  3. Locate, evaluate and use relevant scholarship, literary criticism and cultural commentary, both in print and online.
  4. Engage in public discourse through careful listening, respectful questioning and thoughtful speaking in both formal and informal settings.
  5. Demonstrate in-depth understanding of several areas of study using scientific reasoning to interpret psychological phenomena and their application in a particular sociocultural context.
  6. Understand scientific methods and their application in conducting academic and professional research and to critically evaluate the existing body of knowledge.
  7. Use scientific knowledge in order to understand various facets of behavioral and mental functions and/or can provide clinical or organizational services throughout their occupation in different fields.
  8. Function effectively in a team by assuming different roles and demonstrating effective leadership qualities and project management skills in diverse social settings to accomplish a common goal towards a significant project.
  9. Assess professional, ethical, legal, security and social issues and responsibilities.
  10. Communicate effectively both verbally and in writing with a range of audiences.
  11. Engage in continuing professional development and life-long learning.
